# Schneiter et Fils — company context and 2026 action plan

## Company positioning

Pompes funebres Schneiter et Fils supports families with funeral services, guidance, formalities, tribute, pre-planning, and practical information in difficult moments. The tone must remain dignified, human, calm, and clear.

## Key content points by month

### January — Pre-planning contract

Explain that a pre-planning contract allows people to organise wishes, ceremony setting, and budget in advance. It relieves loved ones when the time comes. Link: https://www.schneiteretfils.ch/prevoyance/

### February — Local association / company supported

Content is pending client information: company name, its role, and why Schneiter et Fils supports it.

### March — What to do in case of death?

Practical steps: contact Schneiter et Fils when ready, gather documents such as ID, family record book, B/C permit, and inform relatives. Phone: 024 425 73 43, available 24/7. Link: https://www.schneiteretfils.ch/que-faire-en-cas-de-deces/

### April — Burial or cremation

Explain neutrally. There is no universally correct choice. Mention wishes of the deceased, beliefs, budget, local possibilities, cemetery availability, timeline, and desired place of remembrance.

### May — Tributes

Tributes preserve memory and offer a respectful space for condolence messages, memories, and information. Link: https://www.schneiteretfils.ch/hommages/

### June — International repatriation

Explain documents, formalities, authorities/consulates, transport organisation, and peace of mind for families. Link: https://www.schneiteretfils.ch/rapatriement/

### July — Summer availability

Message: even during summer, Schneiter et Fils remains available 24/7. Popup: "During the summer period, Schneiter & Fils remains available 24/7 for you."

### August — Memorial Forest in Yverdon

Describe the Memorial Forest at Yverdon-les-Bains cemetery as a wooded, discreet, soothing space for collective remembrance. Mention deposit of ashes without urn/container and optional biodegradable plaque.

### September — Administrative formalities

Mention certificates, documents, official notifications, death certificates, cemetery/crematorium coordination, and drafting a death notice. Emphasize guidance and relief.

### October — Death notice

Explain that a death notice informs relatives with respect and may be published in press or online. It can include ceremony information, reflection, or thanks.

### November — Month of remembrance

Use dignified remembrance ideas: candle, seasonal flowers, memory note, shared photo, symbolic gesture, online tribute.

### December — End-of-year message

Warm but sober message. Mention families for whom the holidays are difficult and 24/7 availability with discretion.
