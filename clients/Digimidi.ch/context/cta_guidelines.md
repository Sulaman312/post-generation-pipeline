# Digimidi — CTA Guidelines

## Core Philosophy: "Invite, Don't Push"
CTAs carry the same veterinary-colleague voice. Principles: **value-first · autonomy-respecting · stage-appropriate · pressure-free · multiple pathways.** Vets are sophisticated buyers — they respond to respect, not pressure.

## CTA Tone Spectrum
- **Soft (awareness):** educational, low commitment, tools/calculators. *"Curious how much time you're spending on…", "See what's possible when…"*
- **Medium (consideration):** solution-focused, demo/trial. *"Schedule a 15-minute walkthrough with Dr. Adam", "See how this works in a real clinic."*
- **Direct (decision):** action-focused, clear next step. *"Book your demo", "Let's explore if Digimidi is right for your practice."*
- **Ultra-soft (relationship):** no product mention, pure value. *"Subscribe to monthly insights", "Save this for when you're ready."*

## Placement
- **End-of-article (always):** most prominent, matched to stage, 2–4 sentences, single clear action.
- **Mid-article (sparingly):** only in 2,000+ word pieces at natural breaks; 1-sentence text link, never interrupts a thought.
- **Opening (rarely):** only for bottom-funnel readers or when the article promises a tool/calculator.

## Phrasing
- **Use:** discovery ("see how," "explore what's possible") · outcome ("calculate your savings," "get your personalized assessment") · low-barrier ("no obligation," "15-minute walkthrough") · credibility ("talk to a practicing vet," "demo with Dr. Adam") · specificity ("20-minute demo," "3-page report").
- **Avoid:** artificial urgency ("limited time," "act now") · guilt/fear ("don't get left behind," "your competitors are…") · vague value ("learn more," "get started") · overpromising ("guaranteed results," "transform overnight") · passive/weak ("feel free to reach out") · corporate jargon ("leverage," "synergize").

## Button/Link Design
- **Button text:** 2–5 words ideal, 8 max; action-oriented and specific ("Calculate Your Time Savings," not "Submit"/"Click Here").
- **Hierarchy:** primary CTA = button with whitespace; secondary = text link with arrow (→); mid-article = text only.

## Multi-CTA Strategy
For long content (2,500+ words) or mixed-readiness readers: pair a **primary action + lower-commitment alternative**. Variants: high/low commitment · different learning styles (watch/read/talk) · decision-stage options.

## Brand-Safe Standards
- **Never say:** false claims ("guaranteed," "100%") · competitor disparagement · clinical claims ("improve patient outcomes," "reduce medical errors" — we improve *efficiency*, vets improve outcomes) · privacy overpromises.
- **Always maintain:** honesty about effort ("implementation takes 2–4 weeks," "there's a learning curve") · realistic outcomes ("save 8–15 hrs weekly," not 30 instantly) · veterinary respect · Swiss context (CHF, geography, multilingual) · value-first framing.

## Templates by Content Type
1. **Problem-awareness:** acknowledge problem → offer assessment tool → no-pressure step.
2. **How-to:** connect learning to action → offer to show application → specific time commitment.
3. **Comparison:** acknowledge decision complexity → expert guidance → two options.
4. **Case study:** connect reader to similar situation → personalized exploration → social proof.
5. **Thought leadership:** extend conversation → deeper resource → subscription.
6. **Bottom-funnel:** acknowledge readiness → clear numbered next steps → remove friction.

## Special Situations
- **Not a fit (non-Swiss/wrong type):** help them find the right solution — build goodwill.
- **Pure thought leadership:** relationship CTA (subscribe) without forcing product.
- **Return visitor / engaged:** offer next logical step ("Email Dr. Adam — no formal demo needed").

## Testing & Metrics
- **Test:** positioning, type (soft/medium/direct), single vs. multi, button vs. link, specific vs. generic copy.
- **Metrics by stage:** awareness → resource engagement; consideration → demo requests/signups; decision → demo bookings/pricing visits.

## Internal Linking
2–4 natural inline text links per article with specific anchor text ("our guide to [topic]," not "click here"). Guide readers across stages (awareness → consideration → decision). End with a "Continue Exploring" related-content module.

## Final Test
Every CTA must pass: *"Would I, as a busy veterinarian, appreciate this — or find it pushy/manipulative?"* If anything other than "appreciate it," rewrite. We build long-term relationships, not chase quick conversions.
