# Veterinary Software — Buyer Personas (Swiss Market)

## 1. The Overwhelmed Clinic Owner — *Dr. Sophie Moreau* (Primary)
**Owner & Lead Vet | 38–52 | Romandie**

- **Context:** Runs a 3–8 vet clinic, manages 8–15 staff, works 50–60 hr/week, juggles clinical + business duties on outdated/disconnected systems.
- **Goals:** More time with patients, modernize to compete with chains, reduce burnout, grow revenue without cutting care quality.
- **Pain points:** 2–3 hrs/day on admin, paper chaos, 15–20% no-shows, revenue leakage from missed reminders, disconnected tools, multilingual gaps.
- **Fears:** Wrong software choice, staff resistance, data loss during migration, learning curve, subscription costs.
- **Wins:** Reclaim 10+ hrs/week, no-shows under 5%, +30% checkup compliance, +15–20% revenue.
- **Tech comfort:** Moderate. **Decision:** Risk-averse, needs clear ROI + peer testimonials, 2–6 month timeline.

## 2. The Tech-Forward Practice Manager — *Léa Durand* (Primary)
**Practice Manager | 32–45 | Geneva, Lausanne, Neuchâtel, Fribourg**

- **Context:** Handles all non-clinical ops (scheduling, billing, comms), reports to owner.
- **Goals:** Modern competitive systems, kill repetitive tasks, improve client experience + online reputation, prove her value.
- **Pain points:** Scheduling across multiple vets, hours of manual reminders, phone bottleneck, after-hours stress, "why no online booking?" complaints, duplicate data entry.
- **Fears:** Blamed for failed rollout, staff pushback, budget rejection, downtime during peak hours.
- **Wins:** −40% calls, −90% manual reminders, 60%+ online booking, Google reviews 4.2 → 4.7+, half the no-shows.
- **Tech comfort:** High. **Decision:** Researches heavily, compares 3–5 tools, builds business case, 1–3 month timeline.

## 3. The Multi-Site Group Executive — *Marc Berger* (Secondary)
**Regional/Operations Director | 45–60 | Multiple Swiss clinics**

- **Context:** Oversees 3–10+ clinics, 30–100+ staff, multiple managers.
- **Goals:** Standardize operations, real-time performance visibility, scale efficiently, data-driven decisions, brand consistency.
- **Pain points:** No centralized view, inconsistent quality, manual multi-site reporting, compliance risk, costly cross-site training.
- **Wins:** Unified dashboard, −25% operational costs, consistent 4.5+ ratings, onboard new clinics in days, 75%+ staff utilization.

## 4. The Young Associate Veterinarian — *Dr. Lucas Petit* (Tertiary)
**Associate Vet | 28–35 | Any Swiss clinic**

- **Context:** 1–5 years post-grad, employee, digital native expecting modern tools.
- **Goals:** Focus on medicine not paperwork, work-life balance, fast record access, modern client comms.
- **Pain points:** 30+ min documentation per patient, illegible handwritten notes, after-hours calls, 20-year-old software.
- **Wins:** Documentation under 5 min/patient, leaving on time, positive client communication ratings.

---

## Key Insights Across Personas

**Universal pain points:** time poverty · client expectation gap · revenue leakage · communication burden · disconnected systems.

**Solutions sought:** all-in-one platform · automation (reminders/alerts/follow-ups) · client self-service · mobile-first · modern comms (WhatsApp/SMS) · AI assistance (voice-to-text, reporting) · Swiss-specific features (multilingual, CHF, compliance) · easy implementation.

**Decision drivers:** ROI clarity · risk mitigation (trials, migration support) · social proof from Swiss clinics · local Swiss support · scalability.
