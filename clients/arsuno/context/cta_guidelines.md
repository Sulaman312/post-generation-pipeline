CTA Guidelines — Arsuno

## 📣 CTA Guidelines — Arsuno.ai

### 1. CTA Philosophy

Arsuno.ai's content exists primarily to educate first, convert second. CTAs should feel like a natural next step for a reader who has found genuine value in the content — not like an interruption or a hard sell. The guiding principle: every CTA should feel like an invitation, not a command. Arsuno.ai is selling a considered, custom, high-trust service. The sales cycle is not impulse-driven. CTAs should move readers toward a conversation or a relationship — not pressure them into an immediate transaction.

### 2. CTA Tone

The tone is: Warm, Direct, and Low-Pressure. Arsuno.ai CTAs should feel like a knowledgeable friend saying "if this resonates with you, here's how we can help" — not a banner ad screaming "BUY NOW."

### ✅ Right Tone ❌ Wrong Tone

Consultative and inviting Urgent and pressuring Outcome-focused Feature-focused Specific to the article's topic Generic across all articles Confident but not pushy Aggressive or salesy

### 3. CTA Placement Rules

Blog Posts & Long-Form Articles Primary CTA Placement: End of Article Only For educational blog content, a single CTA at the end of the article is the default. The reader has consumed the full piece — this is the highest-intent moment. Secondary CTA Placement: Mid-Article (Optional, Contextual Only) A mid-article CTA is acceptable only when there is a highly relevant, natural connection to the content being discussed at that exact point. It should never feel like an advertisement dropped into editorial content. For example: An article about AI document handling might include a mid-article line like: "If you're dealing with this exact challenge, Arsuno.ai has built document automation systems for healthcare and legal teams — you can see how it works here." What to Avoid:

- CTA in the opening section or first 30% of any article
- Multiple hard CTAs in a single blog post
- Pop-up or banner-style CTA language that interrupts the reading experience
- Placing a CTA immediately after a statistic or claim — it reads as manipulative Landing Pages & Feature Pages On landing pages and feature-specific pages, CTAs can appear in multiple places because the reader has arrived with higher intent:
- Above the fold (primary CTA)
- After the feature/benefit breakdown (secondary CTA)
- At the bottom of the page (closing CTA) These CTAs can be slightly more direct in tone than blog CTAs, but still never aggressive. 4. Acceptable CTA Phrasing

### Primary CTAs (Main Conversion Action)

These drive toward a consultation, contact form, or discovery call:

### ✅ Recommended Phrases

- "See how Arsuno.ai can automate your operations — [Get in Touch]"
- "Ready to remove the bottlenecks slowing your team down? [Let's Talk]"
- "Find out what custom AI automation could look like for your business — [Book a Call]"
- "If your team is spending hours on tasks AI could handle, let's change that — [Start the Conversation]"
- "Curious what this would look like for your business? [Get a Free Consultation]" ❌ Phrases to Avoid:
- "Buy Now" / "Sign Up Today" / "Don't Miss Out"
- "Click Here" (non-descriptive, poor for SEO and UX)
- "Get Started for Free" (Arsuno.ai is a custom services business, not a freemium SaaS)
- "Limited Time Offer" / "Act Now" / "Hurry"
- "The best AI automation agency is just one click away"

### Secondary CTAs (Soft Engagement Action)

These drive toward further content consumption or low-commitment next steps:

### ✅ Recommended Phrases

- "Explore how Arsuno.ai handles [specific feature] → [Learn More]"
- "Read how a recruitment agency cut hiring time in half with Arsuno.ai → [See the Case Study]"
- "Want a deeper look at what AI automation could do for your operations? → [Browse Our Features]"
- "Not sure where to start with AI? [Read: How to Identify the Best Automation Opportunities in Your Business]" 5. CTA Specificity Rules CTAs should be contextual to the article topic whenever possible. A generic "Contact Us" CTA at the bottom of every article is weak. A CTA that connects directly to the article's subject matter performs better and feels more authentic. Examples of Contextual CTA Matching: Article Topic Contextual CTA AI for customer support "Want to reduce support ticket volume by 50%? See how Arsuno.ai builds AI chat systems for service teams." Document automation "If your team manually processes documents, there's a faster way. Let's show you what AI-powered extraction looks like." AI for recruitment agencies "Help your team spend less time screening and more time placing. Talk to Arsuno.ai about a custom AI sourcing solution." Business case for AI "Not sure if AI is right for your business yet? Start with a conversation — no commitment required." General AI efficiency "See what 3x operational efficiency could mean for your team. [Book a Discovery Call]" 6. Brand-Safe CTA Messaging Rules These rules protect Arsuno.ai's brand integrity across all content: Never imply a free product or free trial. Arsuno.ai is a custom services company. Any CTA implying free software or a free trial misrepresents the offering. Never create false urgency. No countdown timers, "limited spots available," or time-bound pressure language unless a legitimate promotion exists and is approved. Never over-promise outcomes in CTAs. Outcomes like "Cut your costs by 60%" are credible in a case study context with evidence. They are not appropriate as standalone CTA hooks without context. Always make the next step clear. The CTA should tell the reader exactly what will happen when they click or act. "Book a discovery call" is clear. "Get started" is vague. Maintain the partner positioning. CTAs should reinforce that Arsuno.ai is a collaborative partner, not a vendor pushing a product. The language of partnership ("let's talk," "let's figure out," "we'll look at your specific situation") reinforces this. 7.
