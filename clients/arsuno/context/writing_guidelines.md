### 2. Writing Do's

Do lead with the business problem, not the technology. Readers care about their problem first. Lead with a pain point, a reality, or a provocative insight — then introduce the AI solution as the answer. Do use real numbers and specifics wherever possible. Vague claims erode trust. Specific numbers build it. Use Arsuno.ai's published stats and case study data when referencing outcomes. Do write in second person. Address the reader directly using "you" and "your business." This creates connection and relevance. Do use short paragraphs. No paragraph should exceed 3–4 lines in a blog post. White space is not wasted space — it improves readability and reduces cognitive load. Do use transition sentences between sections. Each section should connect logically to the next. Don't let the reader feel like they are jumping between unrelated topics. Do contextualize AI for the reader's industry. Generic AI content is everywhere. Make it specific — reference recruitment, healthcare, logistics, or whatever sector is most relevant to the piece. Do cite sources or reference data when making industry-level claims. For claims not backed by Arsuno.ai's own results, reference credible external sources (McKinsey, Gartner, Deloitte, MIT, etc.). Do include a clear takeaway at the end of every article. The reader should know exactly what they have learned and what they can do next.

### 3. Writing Don'ts

Don't use AI hype language. Words like "revolutionary," "game-changing," "unprecedented," "cutting-edge," and "transformative" are overused, meaningless, and make content sound generated. Avoid entirely. Don't make the content about Arsuno.ai too early. In blog content especially, the first 70–80% should be purely educational and valuable. Arsuno.ai's positioning comes in naturally at the end, not as an interruption. Don't write feature-first. Never open with a list of what Arsuno.ai does. Always open with what the reader is experiencing, struggling with, or trying to achieve. Don't use passive voice excessively. Passive voice weakens sentences and distances the reader from the action. Weak: "Operational costs are reduced when automation is implemented." Strong: "Automation cuts operational costs — sometimes by 40% or more." Don't use filler phrases. Cut: "In today's fast-paced business environment," "It goes without saying," "At the end of the day," "Needless to say," "Leverage synergies." Don't over-explain AI to the point of condescension. The audience is smart. A brief definition is fine. A three-paragraph lecture on how machine learning works is not. Don't use more than one exclamation mark per article — and only when genuinely warranted. Don't write conclusions that just summarize what was already said. Conclusions should add a forward-looking insight or a decisive takeaway, not simply recap.

### Headline Guidelines

- Use numbers when listing ("5 Ways AI Can…", "3 Signs Your Business…")
- Lead with the reader's outcome, not Arsuno.ai's product
- Avoid clickbait — every headline must be deliverable by the article
- Target 50–65 characters for SEO-friendliness
- Good: "How Recruitment Agencies Are Cutting CV Screening Time in Half With AI"
- Bad: "The Revolutionary AI Solution That Will Transform Your Business Forever"

### Subheadings (H2/H3)

- Should be descriptive and standalone — a reader skimming headers should understand the article's arc
- Avoid vague subheadings like "Introduction," "Overview," or "Conclusion"
- Use outcome-oriented or insight-driven subheadings
- Good: "Why Manual Document Processing Is Costing You More Than You Think"
- Bad: "Document Processing Section"

### Lists & Bullets

- Use only when genuinely listing 3 or more parallel items
- Each bullet should be a complete thought — minimum one full sentence
- Never use bullets to replace prose that would read better as a paragraph
- Maximum 5–6 bullets per list before breaking into subheadings

### Bold Text 

- Use sparingly — only for genuinely critical phrases or key terms
- Never bold entire sentences or decoratively
- One or two bolded phrases per section maximum

### 6. SEO Preferences

Primary Goal: Rank for mid-funnel, intent-rich keywords that reflect what Arsuno.ai's ICP searches when they are problem-aware and solution-seeking. Target Keyword Types:

- Problem-aware: "how to reduce manual work in operations," "automate customer support small business"
- Solution-aware: "AI automation for recruitment," "custom AI chatbot for healthcare"
- Comparison: "AI automation agency vs SaaS tools," "custom AI vs off-the-shelf software"
- Outcome-based: "reduce operational costs with AI," "scale business without hiring" SEO Do's:
- Place primary keyword naturally in H1, first 100 words, one H2, and meta description
- Use semantic variations and related terms throughout — never keyword stuff
- Write meta descriptions as a benefit-led summary of the article, 150–160 characters
- Use internal links to relevant Arsuno.ai feature pages or related blog posts
- Use descriptive alt text for any images or visuals
- Structure content so featured snippet capture is possible (direct answer to a question within the first 40–60 words of a section) SEO Don'ts:
- Never sacrifice readability for keyword density
- Don't target purely informational top-of-funnel keywords that attract readers with no buying intent
- Don't repeat the exact same keyword phrase more than 3–4 times in an article
