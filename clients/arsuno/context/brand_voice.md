## ✍ Writing Guidelines — Arsuno.ai

### 1. Voice & Tone

### Brand Voice (The Personality Behind Every Word)

Arsuno.ai's voice is that of a knowledgeable, results-driven partner — not a pushy salesperson, not an academic researcher, and not a hype machine. Think of it as a senior AI consultant who sits across the table from a business owner, speaks plainly, respects their time, and backs every claim with evidence. Four Core Voice Attributes: Confident, Not Arrogant Arsuno.ai has proof — published results, real case studies, real client outcomes. The writing should carry that confidence naturally, without bragging. Let the results do the talking. Right: "Businesses that automate document processing with AI typically cut processing time by over 60%." Wrong: "We are the best AI automation agency and no one does it better than us." Clear, Not Dumbed Down The audience includes founders, ops managers, and business owners — intelligent people who are not necessarily AI engineers. Write as if explaining to a smart non-technical professional. Avoid jargon unless it's immediately explained. Right: "AI can automatically extract key data from invoices, contracts, and intake forms — no manual entry needed." Wrong: "Our transformer-based NLP pipeline performs entity recognition and structured data extraction across heterogeneous document formats." Human, Not Corporate The content should feel written by a real person who understands business pain. Avoid stiff, formal language. Use contractions. Use "you." Address the reader's reality directly. Right: "If your team is spending half their week on tasks a machine could handle, that's not a people problem — it's an operations problem." Wrong: "Organizations seeking operational optimization may consider implementing AI-driven workflow automation solutions." Practical, Not Theoretical Every piece of content should leave the reader with something actionable — an insight, a framework, a decision to make. Abstract AI philosophy has no place here. Right: "Here's how a recruitment agency reduced CV screening time by 70% using an AI shortlisting model." Wrong: "AI represents the next paradigm shift in how humanity will approach knowledge work in the 21st century."
